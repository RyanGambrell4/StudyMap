# Brand Context

> This file is auto-loaded by all blog sub-skills. Last updated: 2026-07-15.

## Audience

- **Primary**: College students (freshman through senior) at US 4-year universities — especially students struggling with grades, overwhelmed by coursework, or looking to study smarter before exams
- **Secondary**: Pre-med, nursing, law school, and grad-school test prep students (MCAT, NCLEX, GRE, LSAT, GMAT, USMLE)
- **Expertise**: Beginner to intermediate
- **Active problems**:
  - Cramming the night before and still failing
  - Spending hours studying but nothing sticks
  - GPA slipping and not knowing how to recover
  - Feeling overwhelmed by STEM course volume
  - Procrastinating because studying feels pointless
- **Common misconceptions**:
  - Re-reading notes is effective studying
  - Highlighting is a study technique
  - Studying more hours is the only answer
  - ChatGPT is a good study companion (it doesn't quiz you)
  - Cramming works if done long enough

## Positioning

- **Mission**: Give every college student the study system that high-performing students use, powered by AI so it works with their real course material
- **Distinctive POV**: Studying smarter is a learnable skill, not a personality trait. Most students fail because they use passive review instead of active recall.
- **What we are NOT**: A tutoring service, homework cheating tool, flashcard app, or generic AI chatbot. NOT affiliated with studyedge.com (the tutoring company).
- **Competitors**:
  - Quizlet: StudyEdge AI generates flashcards from YOUR notes automatically — Quizlet requires manual creation
  - Anki: Same spaced-repetition science but zero setup friction and AI-powered card generation
  - ChatGPT: ChatGPT explains things; StudyEdge AI quizzes you — retrieval practice IS learning
