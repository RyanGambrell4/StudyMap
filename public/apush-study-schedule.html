APUSH Study Schedule

The 8-week AP U.S. History plan. Weighted to the College Board period distribution, with DBQ and LEQ practice building from week 3 and two full-length practice exams. Print it, put it on your calendar, or let the AI adapt it.

Exam format reminder: 55 MCQs (55 min), 3 SAQs (40 min), 1 DBQ (60 min), 1 LEQ (40 min). Section 1 (MCQ + SAQ) is 60% of your score. Section 2 (DBQ + LEQ) is 40%. Total exam: 3 hours 15 minutes.

Period weights on the APUSH exam

The College Board publishes approximate weight ranges. Use these to allocate more review time to higher-weight periods.

  • Period 1 (1491-1607): 4-6%
  • Period 2 (1607-1754): 6-8%
  • Period 3 (1754-1800): 10-17%
  • Period 4 (1800-1848): 10-17%
  • Period 5 (1844-1877): 10-17% (and frequent DBQ topic)
  • Period 6 (1865-1898): 10-17%
  • Period 7 (1890-1945): 10-17%
  • Period 8 (1945-1980): 10-17%
  • Period 9 (1980-present): 4-6%

The 4-week compressed version

  • Week 1: Periods 1-4. Skim, 20 MCQs per period pair.
  • Week 2: Periods 5-7. Two SAQs. Practice Exam 1.
  • Week 3: Periods 8-9 + DBQ intensive. Practice Exam 2.
  • Week 4: Weak-area targeting Mon-Wed. LEQ practice Thu. Rest Fri-Sun.

What most APUSH students get wrong

They over-invest in Period 1 and Period 9 (combined weight only 8-12%) and under-invest in Periods 3-7 (combined weight often 60%+). Follow the weights.

They memorize dates and neglect themes. APUSH is thematic: politics, work/exchange/technology, culture/society, migration, geography/environment, America in the world. The DBQ and LEQ are graded on thematic argument, not date recall.

They skip the LEQ practice because "I already do DBQs." The two skills are related but not identical. Write at least three LEQs before exam day.
