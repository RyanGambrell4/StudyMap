AP Biology Study Schedule

The 8-week plan for AP Bio. Front-loads Units 3 and 6 (the two highest-weight units), builds in data analysis practice (a common weak spot), and hits two full-length practice exams.

Exam format reminder: 60 MCQs (90 min) plus 6 FRQs (90 min). Two long FRQs (worth about 8-10 points each) and four short FRQs (worth 4 points each). Section 1 and 2 are each 50% of your score.

Unit weights on the AP Biology exam

The College Board publishes approximate weight ranges. Weight your study time accordingly.

  • Unit 1 (Chemistry of Life): 8-11%
  • Unit 2 (Cell Structure & Function): 10-13%
  • Unit 3 (Cellular Energetics): 12-16% (front-load this)
  • Unit 4 (Cell Communication & Cell Cycle): 10-15%
  • Unit 5 (Heredity): 8-11%
  • Unit 6 (Gene Expression & Regulation): 12-16% (front-load this)
  • Unit 7 (Natural Selection): 13-20%
  • Unit 8 (Ecology): 10-15%

What most AP Bio students get wrong

They over-study memorizable content (photosynthesis diagrams) and under-study experimental design. Half the exam requires you to interpret data, not recall facts. Reserve at least one full week for graph reading and experimental analysis.

They skip the AP Bio equations sheet. You get one on exam day. It has statistical formulas (chi-square, standard error), Hardy-Weinberg, and rate calculations. Practice with it in front of you every session from week 3 on.

They wait too long to write long FRQs. The two long FRQs are worth roughly half your FRQ points. Practice at least one per week starting week 4.

The 4-week compressed version

  • Week 1: Units 1, 2, 3 (through cellular energetics). 15 MCQs per unit.
  • Week 2: Units 4, 5, 6. Practice Exam 1 at end of week.
  • Week 3: Units 7, 8. Data analysis practice. Practice Exam 2.
  • Week 4: Weak-area targeting Mon-Wed. Rest Thu-Sun.
