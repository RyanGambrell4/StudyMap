Admissions context for Georgetown University

Georgetown University is one of the most selective private universities in the country. The 12% acceptance rate puts it in the highly selective tier alongside schools like Vanderbilt, Tufts, and Emory. Georgetown has a reputation for political science, international affairs, and pre-law tracks that attracts a specific and highly competitive applicant pool. The average admitted student has consistently strong academic credentials with a 4.0+ unweighted GPA.

GPA by program

Georgetown College (Arts and Sciences): Most broadly accessible path into Georgetown. 4.0+ unweighted with rigorous AP/IB coursework typical.

Walsh School of Foreign Service: Among the most competitive undergraduate international affairs programs in the world. Average admitted student GPA is 4.0+ with exceptional extracurricular engagement in international topics.

McDonough School of Business: Competitive. 4.0+ unweighted typical with quantitative strength and demonstrated business or entrepreneurship involvement.

School of Nursing and Health Studies: Competitive. 3.9+ unweighted typical with strong science performance through AP Biology and Chemistry.

What if my GPA is below the target?

Below 3.9 at Georgetown is a significant challenge given the 12% acceptance rate. Exceptional extracurricular distinction, compelling personal narrative, and demonstrated intellectual engagement in Georgetown's core areas can compensate to some degree, but the academic floor is high. Treat Georgetown as a reach if you are below the 25th percentile of admitted students.
