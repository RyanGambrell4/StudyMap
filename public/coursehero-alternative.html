Why Students Switch

A search engine for class notes is not a study plan

Course Hero solved the problem of finding old study guides on the internet. That problem mattered in 2010. The problem that matters now is staying organized across five courses with eight exams and finals coming up in three weeks.

Course-specific content, fresh

You describe what you are studying. The AI generates flashcards, practice problems, and concept explanations calibrated to that material. No hunting for a usable study guide on a 12-million-document index, hoping the one you click is for the same topic.

A real schedule, not a search bar

Add courses and exam dates. StudyEdge AI builds a weekly plan with specific sessions: what to do, how long, which topic. Course Hero has no concept of when your exams are.

An AI tutor with context

The tutor knows what class you are in, what topic is up next, and what you have already struggled with. It can re-explain a concept three different ways, generate practice problems on it, and quiz you until you have it down.

Active recall every session

Reading uploaded study guides is passive. Recall practice is what actually moves the needle on exam performance. Every StudyEdge AI session builds in retrieval prompts, brain dumps, and self-testing.

Grade Hub

Enter your grades, see what you need on remaining work to land an A, B, or whatever target you set. Course Hero does not know what your grade is. StudyEdge AI builds the plan around it.

Adaptive coaching

Bombed a quiz? The schedule adjusts. Coasting in another course? Hours move where they are needed. The plan responds to how you are actually doing, not what you wished was true at the start of the semester.
