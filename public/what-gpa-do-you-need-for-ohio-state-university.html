The honest answer on GPA for Ohio State University

Ohio State University's overall acceptance rate of around 52% makes it one of the more accessible major research universities, but the average admitted student still has a strong academic record. The middle 50 percent of admitted students typically have unweighted GPAs between 3.5 and 3.9. Programs within the Fisher College of Business and the College of Engineering are considerably more selective than the university average.

GPA by program

College of Arts and Sciences: Most accessible. GPAs of 3.4-3.6 unweighted can be competitive with strong test scores and extracurriculars.

Fisher College of Business: More selective. Admitted students average 3.7+ unweighted with strong math performance. Direct freshman admission is competitive.

College of Engineering: Competitive. 3.7-3.9+ unweighted typical. Computer Science and Electrical Engineering programs attract the strongest applicants.

Nursing: Competitive. 3.6+ unweighted typical with strong science GPA.

What if my GPA is below the target?

Below 3.5 unweighted at Ohio State does not automatically disqualify an applicant, particularly for less selective programs. Strong upward GPA trends, test scores, and compelling extracurriculars can compensate. For the most competitive programs like engineering or business, falling below 3.6 makes direct admission significantly harder.
