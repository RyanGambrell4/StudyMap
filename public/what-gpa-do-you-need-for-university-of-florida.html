The honest answer on GPA for University of Florida

University of Florida is one of the most competitive public universities in the Southeast. Despite its 31% overall acceptance rate, the average admitted in-state student has very strong academic credentials. The weighted GPA average for admitted students is approximately 4.2, which translates to around 3.85 unweighted with rigorous AP/IB coursework. UF has become increasingly selective as its national reputation has grown.

GPA by program

College of Liberal Arts and Sciences: The broadest college at UF. Competitive admits typically need 3.7+ unweighted with strong test scores.

Warrington College of Business: Highly competitive. Direct freshman admission requires strong academics and extracurricular achievement. 3.85+ unweighted is typical.

Herbert Wertheim College of Engineering: Competitive. 3.8+ unweighted with strong math and science preparation. Computer Science is the most selective program.

College of Health and Human Performance / Nursing: Competitive. 3.7+ unweighted typical with strong science performance for health-related programs.

What if my GPA is below the target?

Below 3.7 at UF makes admission significantly harder, especially for in-state applicants who make up the majority of the applicant pool. Strong test scores (ACT 33+, SAT 1450+) can help compensate. Out-of-state applicants below 3.8 face very long odds given that UF heavily prioritizes Florida residents.
