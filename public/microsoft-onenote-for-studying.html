Why Students Switch

OneNote is a capture tool. Studying requires something more.

Reviewing organized notes is better than reviewing disorganized ones. But reviewing notes, however organized, is still passive. Retention requires active recall, spaced repetition, and deliberate practice, none of which OneNote provides.

A study schedule, not a notebook

OneNote holds your notes. StudyEdge AI tells you when to review which topics, how long each session should run, and what exam you are working toward. The schedule is the thing OneNote cannot give you.

Active recall, not passive re-reading

Re-reading organized notes produces familiarity, not retrieval. StudyEdge AI runs active recall sessions where you produce information from memory, then check it. That is the mechanism that builds long-term retention.

AI-generated practice questions

From your course topics, StudyEdge AI generates practice questions and flashcards calibrated to what your professor tests. OneNote holds what your professor covered. StudyEdge AI prepares you to be tested on it.

Grade Hub and target tracking

Enter your scores and Grade Hub tells you exactly what you need on remaining assignments to reach your target grade. OneNote does not know you have a 78% in Organic Chemistry and need an 82 on the final.

AI Tutor when you get stuck

You can re-read a OneNote page ten times and still not understand why the Krebs cycle works the way it does. The AI Tutor in StudyEdge AI explains concepts step by step and answers follow-up questions.

Adapts as you fall behind

If you miss a week of studying, the plan adjusts. OneNote just sits there with unchanged notes. StudyEdge AI rebuilds the schedule around the time you have left before the exam.
