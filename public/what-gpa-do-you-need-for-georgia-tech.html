Admissions context for Georgia Tech

Georgia Tech is one of the most selective public universities in the country. Its 16% acceptance rate puts it in the same tier as private schools like Georgetown or Vanderbilt. The average admitted student has a 4.0 unweighted GPA or better with an extremely rigorous high school course load. Georgia Tech is primarily a STEM institution — engineers, scientists, and quantitatively-focused business students make up the majority of the student body.

GPA by program

College of Computing: Among the most competitive CS programs in the US. Admitted students virtually all have near-perfect GPAs and extensive CS project portfolios or competition experience. Direct CS admission from high school is extremely competitive.

College of Engineering: All engineering disciplines are selective. Average admitted students have 4.0+ unweighted with maximum rigor in AP math and science. Aerospace, Electrical, and Chemical Engineering are well-known and attract very strong applicants.

Scheller College of Business: Competitive. 3.9+ unweighted typical. Quantitative focus makes strong math performance a key differentiator.

Ivan Allen College of Liberal Arts: Most accessible at Georgia Tech. Still typically requires 3.7+ unweighted given Tech's overall selectivity.

What if my GPA is below the target?

Below 3.9 at Georgia Tech is a significant obstacle. Given the 16% acceptance rate, applications below the 25th percentile of admitted students need exceptional distinguishing factors to compensate. Strong STEM competition awards, research experience, or other demonstrable technical achievement can help, but GPA near 4.0 is effectively a floor for competitive applicants.

Improving your GPA during college

Georgia Tech's academic rigor is legendary. The adjustment from high school to Tech is significant even for students with perfect high school records. Building structured study habits and starting exam preparation weeks in advance rather than days is essential for GPA maintenance.
