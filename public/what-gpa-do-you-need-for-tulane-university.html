Admissions context for Tulane University

Tulane University has become one of the most selective universities in the South over the past decade. The 13% acceptance rate puts it in a comparable tier to Georgetown and Notre Dame. Tulane has particular strengths in public health, business (A. B. Freeman School), and its distinctive interdisciplinary liberal arts programs. The university's New Orleans location is a meaningful draw for a certain type of applicant, and the resulting self-selection produces a competitive pool.

GPA by program

School of Liberal Arts: Most accessible path into Tulane. 3.5-3.7+ unweighted with strong extracurriculars competitive.

A. B. Freeman School of Business: Competitive. 3.6-3.8+ unweighted with quantitative performance and demonstrated business or leadership experience.

School of Public Health and Tropical Medicine: The strongest public health undergraduate program in the Southeast. 3.6-3.8+ unweighted with health-related extracurricular engagement.

School of Science and Engineering: Competitive. 3.6-3.8+ unweighted with STEM AP coursework.

What if my GPA is below the target?

Below 3.4 at Tulane is a significant obstacle given the 13% acceptance rate. Strong essays that connect genuinely to Tulane's specific culture and mission can carry real weight in the holistic process. Tulane applicants who demonstrate a real understanding of what makes Tulane distinct tend to outperform their GPA profile in admission outcomes.
