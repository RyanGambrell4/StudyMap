What It Does

Built for the finance student's workload, end to end

Six features that exist specifically because generic study apps cannot handle the quantitative and conceptual demands of undergraduate and graduate finance programs.

Quantitative problem practice sessions

Corporate finance, investments, and derivatives exams are majority calculation. StudyEdge AI structures your sessions around timed problem practice — NPV and IRR analysis, bond duration, portfolio variance, options Greeks — so you build the calculation fluency that exams require, not just conceptual familiarity.

AI tutor for valuation and theory

Ask the AI tutor to walk through a DCF model, explain WACC in the context of capital structure, or clarify the difference between the Gordon Growth Model and a multistage dividend discount model. The tutor explains both the formula and the financial logic behind it.

Prerequisite tracking across finance courses

Derivatives pricing assumes options theory. Financial modeling assumes corporate finance. Fixed income assumes time-value-of-money fluency. StudyEdge AI maps these dependencies and queues refresher sessions on upstream concepts when your current course needs them.

Spaced flashcard review for formulas and definitions

CAPM, Modigliani-Miller propositions, put-call parity, the Sharpe ratio — finance has hundreds of formulas and framework definitions to internalize. AI-generated flashcards with spaced repetition ensure you recall them under exam pressure, not just during review.

Adaptive topic targeting

When practice questions reveal weakness in options pricing or ratio analysis, StudyEdge AI increases session time on those topics automatically. You stop spending time on what you already know and concentrate effort where it moves your grade the most.

Grade and deadline tracking across all courses

Finance programs often run corporate finance, investments, and accounting simultaneously. StudyEdge AI tracks your current grade in each, shows which course poses the greatest GPA risk, and adjusts your weekly plan accordingly — no manual scheduling required.

Why finance students need more than flashcards and re-reading notes

Finance exams are built around multi-step problems that require you to recall a formula, choose the right inputs, execute the calculation correctly, and interpret the result — all under time pressure. Students who spend their study time re-reading slides and making flashcards often find themselves stuck when they encounter a problem they have not actually solved before. StudyEdge AI structures every session around active problem solving first, with the AI tutor available to explain any step you cannot work through, so exam day feels like practice rather than a surprise.

How a typical finance student's week looks in StudyEdge AI

You add your finance courses — Corporate Finance, Investments, and Financial Modeling — along with midterm dates and case study deadlines.
