Built for the biology majors workload, end to end

Six features that exist specifically because the generic study apps do not handle biology majors courses well.

Process maps, not vocab decks

Every biology process (glycolysis, Krebs, transcription, mitosis) gets a one-page map you redraw from memory. Map building, not term memorization.

Predict-what-happens drills

What if cofactor X is missing? What if step Y is blocked? The drill format exams use, run in the app.

Genetics calculation practice

Punnett squares, dihybrid crosses, chi-square. Drilled the way exam questions appear, not just defined.

Diagram-from-memory practice

Cell organelles, the nephron, the synapse. The labeling task that lab practical and lecture exams both use.

Multi-course allocation

Bio majors usually take 2 to 3 simultaneous bio courses on top of chemistry and physics. The planner balances by exam proximity.

Pre-med-friendly tracking

If you are biology pre-med, the Grade Hub computes BCPM GPA separately from overall, so you see what med schools see.

Why biology majors burn out on flashcard apps

Vocab is the smallest part of biology success. Process understanding, prediction skills, and integration across courses are what produce A grades. How a typical biology majors week looks in StudyEdge AI

You add your courses, exam dates, and the topics you are weakest on. The system generates a weekly schedule with specific sessions: which course, which topic, what method, how long.
