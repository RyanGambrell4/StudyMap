Admissions context for Vanderbilt University

Vanderbilt University has become one of the most selective universities in the United States, with acceptance rates that have dropped dramatically over the past decade. The 6.7% acceptance rate puts it alongside schools like Johns Hopkins, Brown, and Dartmouth. Academic excellence is necessary but not sufficient for Vanderbilt admission. The average admitted student has both exceptional grades and significant extracurricular achievement.

GPA by program

College of Arts and Science: Most general path into Vanderbilt. 3.9+ unweighted with maximum rigor coursework typical.

Owen Graduate School of Management (undergraduate pre-business): Extremely competitive. Most Vanderbilt students interested in business enter through A&S and then apply to Vanderbilt Business School for graduate work.

School of Engineering: Competitive. 3.9+ unweighted with strong STEM preparation through AP Calculus and science courses.

Peabody College (Education and Human Development): Competitive. 3.8+ unweighted typical.

What if my GPA is below the target?

Below 3.8 at Vanderbilt is a significant obstacle given the 6.7% acceptance rate. The admissions process is genuinely holistic, meaning that remarkable achievements outside the classroom can sometimes compensate for a slightly lower GPA. But the academic floor for competitive applicants is very high.
