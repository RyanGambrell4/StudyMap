Why Students Switch

A K-12 lesson library is not a college study plan

Khan Academy is the right tool for learning a single concept in isolation. It is not the right tool for managing a semester of work across multiple courses with real deadlines.

College-level material, on demand

The AI generates explanations, examples, and practice problems for any topic in your course, from biochem to econometrics. You are not limited to whatever made it into the Khan Academy library.

A schedule built around your semester

Add courses and exam dates. StudyEdge AI builds a weekly plan with specific sessions covering specific topics, prioritized by what is coming up. Khan Academy has no concept of when your finals are.

An AI tutor with full context

The tutor knows what course you are in, which topics you have struggled with before, and what is coming up next. It can answer your question, generate a similar problem to make sure you got it, and add the topic to your review queue.

Active recall, not passive videos

Watching a video is the lowest-yield form of studying. StudyEdge AI builds in retrieval prompts, brain dumps, and self-testing, so every session is actively producing information from memory.

Grade Hub

Enter your grades. The system tells you exactly what you need on remaining work to land an A, B, or your specific target. Khan Academy does not track your GPA. StudyEdge AI builds the plan around it.

Adaptive, not fixed

Most courses online walk you through a fixed lesson sequence. StudyEdge AI changes the plan based on what you bombed, what you crushed, and what is coming up next.
