Admissions context for Case Western Reserve University

Case Western Reserve University is a private research university in Cleveland, Ohio with exceptional strength in engineering, medicine, and law. The 27% acceptance rate is more accessible than many peer private universities, but the academic profile of admitted students is consistently strong. CWRU is particularly notable for its biomedical engineering program (consistently top 5 nationally) and its direct-admit medical program partnership with Case Western Reserve School of Medicine.

GPA by program

College of Arts and Sciences: 3.6-3.8+ unweighted competitive for most programs.

Case School of Engineering: Competitive. 3.7-3.9+ unweighted with strong STEM coursework. Biomedical engineering is the flagship program and most selective.

Weatherhead School of Management: 3.6-3.8+ unweighted with quantitative performance.

Pre-Medical Programs (PPSP): CWRU's Pre-Professional Scholars Program offers conditional acceptance to Case Western Reserve School of Medicine. This is extremely competitive and requires near-perfect academic records.

What if my GPA is below the target?

Below 3.5 at CWRU begins to limit access to the most competitive programs. For the direct-admit medical program (PPSP), nothing below approximately 3.9+ is competitive. General arts and sciences admission remains accessible in the 3.4-3.5 range with strong supporting application components.

Improving your GPA once enrolled
