# Who Uses StudyEdge AI

## Primary Audience

College and university students at any level (freshman through graduate school) who need to manage multiple courses, track grades, and study more effectively.

## By Major / Program

### Pre-Med Students
Managing BCPM GPA across Biology, Chemistry, Organic Chemistry, Biochemistry, and Physics. Using grade tracking to protect GPA and identify which courses need the most attention. MCAT prep scheduling.

### Engineering Students
Managing problem-heavy STEM courses simultaneously. Grade calculator to know exactly what exam score is needed. Study scheduling for thermodynamics, circuits, signals, and other high-difficulty courses.

### Nursing Students
Managing clinical coursework alongside heavy science courses (A&P, Pharmacology, Pathophysiology). NCLEX-style active recall practice built into sessions.

### Business Students
Managing case studies, group projects, finance, marketing, and strategy courses. Grade tracking across multiple exam-heavy courses. GPA monitoring for recruiting cutoffs.

### Computer Science Students
Managing algorithms, data structures, theory, and math courses. Grade calculation for each course. Active recall for CS concepts and theory exam preparation.

### Medical / Graduate Students
USMLE Step 1 prep scheduling. Biochemistry and Pharmacology spaced repetition. Multi-month study schedule management.

## By Use Case

### Finals Prep
Calculate the exact score needed on each final. Build a 10-14 day countdown schedule. Session blueprints that cover the highest-yield material first.

### GPA Recovery
Identify which courses can realistically improve GPA given credit load. Model scenarios (what if I ace this course vs. get a B+?). Triage study time based on GPA impact per hour.

### Weekly Scheduling
Convert a course list with exam dates into a weekly calendar with study sessions assigned to the right subjects on the right days.

### Semester-Long Management
Track all assignments and deadlines from syllabus import through finals. Never lose track of grade weights, drop policies, or upcoming exams.
