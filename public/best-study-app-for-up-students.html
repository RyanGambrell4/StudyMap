For UP Students

UP's scale means no one manages your study schedule for you.

At a university with over 50,000 students, the onus is on you to keep track of where you stand across your modules. StudyEdge AI gives you the tools to do that without spending an hour every Sunday on a spreadsheet.

Health sciences block tracking

UP's Faculty of Health Sciences covers medicine, dentistry, pharmacy, physiotherapy, nursing, and dietetics. Each programme runs block-based assessments with multiple components. StudyEdge AI builds block-specific study plans, runs spaced repetition sessions, and tracks your standing across components so you know what to prioritise before each summative.

Engineering EBIT workload management

EBIT students at UP manage concurrent tutorials, lab sessions, design assignments, and class tests alongside semester exams. The StudyEdge AI schedule builder accounts for every assessment type so your weekly plan reflects the full workload, not just the exam calendar.

Law faculty preparation

The UP Faculty of Law runs LLB and BCom Law programmes with a high volume of case law, legislation, and applied legal reasoning. The AI tutor generates problem questions and hypotheticals, walks through legal argument construction, and helps you practise the analytical structure that UP law exams reward.

Economic and management sciences

EMS students at UP manage accounting, economics, business management, and marketing simultaneously. Grade Hub tracks each module separately so you can see at a glance where your marks are strong and where they need work, well before the final exam window.

Running percentage visibility

UP assessments are weighted across the semester. Grade Hub shows your running weighted average in each course, not just a list of marks, so you can see your actual standing. Enter your assessments at the start of semester and update as results come in.

Exam period balance across all modules

UP's exam period can be intense, with multiple papers in a short window. The exam planner balances your remaining study time across all your modules by exam date and current standing, so your heaviest attention goes to the subject where you need it most.
