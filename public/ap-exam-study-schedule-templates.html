The generic 8-week AP schedule

If your subject is not in the list above, use this shell. It works for any content-heavy AP subject: Bio, Chem, APUSH, World, Environmental Science, Psychology.

8-week AP review shell

Working backwards from exam day. Adjust weeks 1 to 3 to match your slowest unit.
Week Focus What to actually do
Week 1FoundationsSkim the full CED (Course and Exam Description). List every unit and rate your comfort 1 to 5. Read one review chapter for the two lowest-comfort units.
Week 2Early unitsDeep review of Units 1 to 3. One flashcard deck per unit. Two 30-minute active recall sessions per unit.
Week 3Mid unitsDeep review of Units 4 to 6. Same structure as week 2. First timed 15-question multiple choice set at end of week.
Week 4Late unitsDeep review of Units 7 to end. First full free-response question under timed conditions.
Week 5First full-length practiceTake one official practice exam under real conditions. Score it. Identify the three weakest question types.
Week 6Targeted weaknessEvery session targets one of the three weak types. Two FRQs per session. Redo the questions you got wrong on the practice exam.
Week 7Second full-length practiceTake another official practice exam. Compare to week 5. Adjust final week's plan based on remaining weak areas.
Week 8SharpeningShort, spaced review sessions across all units. No new material. Two FRQs per day for the first four days. Last three days: rest, sleep, review flashcards only.

The single most important rule: Weeks 1 to 4 build content. Weeks 5 to 8 build test-taking. Students who skip the practice-exam phase almost always underperform their content mastery, because they have not practiced the format.

What actually makes an AP study schedule work

Most AP schedules on the internet are pretty PDFs. They look good on Pinterest and get abandoned by week 2. Here is what the templates on this page do differently.

They front-load your weakest units

Students naturally review the units they already know because it feels productive. It is not. The schedule should force you to spend the most time on your lowest-comfort units, not your highest. Rate every unit 1 to 5 in week 1 and use that ranking to weight your calendar.

They separate content review from format practice

Reading a review chapter and taking a timed multiple choice section are two different skills. The first four weeks are content. The last four are format. Mixing them too early is the reason many students score below their content knowledge.

They schedule two full-length practice exams

One practice exam tells you how you did. Two tell you what you fixed. The College Board publishes free practice exams; use them. Everything else in this schedule exists to move the delta between practice one and practice two upward.

They fade to sleep in the last three days

The most common mistake in the final week is a panicked all-nighter. Sleep debt destroys the exact working memory you need for FRQs. The templates go light in the last 72 hours on purpose. If the plan feels too easy that week, it is working.

Frequently asked questions

How many weeks should you study for an AP exam?
For high-content AP exams (APUSH, AP Bio, AP Chem, AP World), 8 weeks of focused review is the sweet spot. For math-based AP exams (AP Calc AB, AP Stats, AP Physics 1), 6 weeks works if you already understood the class content. Below 4 weeks, you are cramming rather than studying, and retention drops sharply.
How many hours per week for an AP exam?
Aim for 4 to 6 hours per week across 3 to 4 sessions, ramping to 8 to 10 hours in the last two weeks. More important than raw hours is spaced practice, reviewing each unit at least three times across separate days. See the spaced repetition vs cramming comparison for the research on why.

When are the 2026 AP exams?

What if I only have 4 weeks?
Skip weeks 1 and 2 of the 8-week template, cut week 6 in half, and keep both practice exams. You will lose depth on the first three units, so pair the compressed schedule with an AI tutor for the units you had to skim. Better than nothing.
