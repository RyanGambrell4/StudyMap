For UCT Students

UCT's intensity requires a study system that actually keeps up.

At UCT, the margin between passing and failing a module, or between a lower second and an upper second, can be as small as a few percentage points. That kind of precision requires tracking, not guessing.

Know your percentage at all times

UCT uses a percentage grading system. 50% passes. 60-69% earns a lower second. 70-74% is an upper second. 75%+ is distinction. Grade Hub tracks your running mark in each course and shows you exactly what you need on remaining assessments to cross the threshold you are aiming for.

EBE workloads require a real system

Engineering, Built Environment, and IT students at UCT manage lab reports, tutorials, class tests, and final papers simultaneously. StudyEdge AI builds a schedule that accounts for every assessment, not just exam dates, so you are not scrambling to submit a lab report while also cramming for a class test.

Health Sciences block preparation

MBChB and allied health students study in blocks. Each block covers dense anatomy, physiology, and clinical content. StudyEdge AI builds block-specific plans and runs spaced repetition sessions designed around the volume of content you need to retain before OSCEs and block exams.

Commerce and law exam preparation

UCT's Commerce Faculty and Law School expect structured, precise answers. StudyEdge AI's AI tutor generates practice essays, case study questions, and multi-part calculation problems in accounting, economics, and commercial law, all calibrated to the kind of questions UCT examiners actually set.

Exam season without chaos

UCT's semester-end exam periods compress multiple modules into a short window. StudyEdge AI's exam planner takes all your exam dates, assigns daily study targets to each subject, and counts down the days so you have a clear plan rather than a generalised panic.

Active recall that actually works

Re-reading notes does not prepare you for UCT exams. The AI tutor runs retrieval sessions where you produce answers from memory, then checks them. This is the evidence-based method that translates to actual exam performance, not just the feeling of being prepared.
