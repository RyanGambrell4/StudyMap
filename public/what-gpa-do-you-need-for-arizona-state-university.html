Admissions context for Arizona State University

Arizona State University intentionally pursues an access-oriented admissions philosophy. It is the largest public university in the US by enrollment and has built a research infrastructure that rivals much more selective institutions. The 88% acceptance rate reflects this philosophy. Practically, most students with a 2.5+ unweighted GPA and a standard high school diploma can gain admission. Barrett Honors College and specific competitive programs require meaningfully higher credentials.

GPA by program

General undergraduate admission: 2.5+ unweighted GPA with standard high school courses is typically sufficient.

W. P. Carey School of Business: More competitive. 3.0-3.3+ unweighted for direct freshman admission. Quantitative coursework strength matters.

Ira A. Fulton Schools of Engineering: 3.0-3.5+ unweighted depending on specific program. Computer Science is the most competitive at 3.4+.

Barrett Honors College: Selective within ASU. 3.5+ unweighted with strong test scores (ACT 25+ or SAT 1200+) typical. Requires separate application.

What if my GPA is below the target?

Below 2.5 at ASU, the university offers conditional admission pathways through ASU Prep or community college articulation agreements. The university's access philosophy means that almost no qualified student is completely closed out — there is typically a pathway to enrollment.
