Admissions context for Boston University

Boston University has become increasingly selective in recent years. The 19% acceptance rate reflects a strong national and international reputation across its schools. BU's location in Boston — one of the most concentrated university markets in the world — means it attracts applicants who want the urban academic environment alongside rigorous academics. The average admitted student has approximately 3.8+ unweighted GPA.

GPA by program

College of Arts and Sciences: Accessible path into BU. 3.6-3.8+ unweighted competitive.

Questrom School of Business: More selective. 3.8-4.0+ unweighted typical with strong quantitative coursework and extracurricular achievement.

College of Engineering: Competitive. 3.8+ unweighted with strong AP math and science preparation.

College of Communication (journalism and media): 3.6+ unweighted with demonstrated writing and media experience typical.

Sargent College of Health and Rehabilitation Sciences: Competitive. 3.7+ unweighted for health-related programs with strong science performance.

What if my GPA is below the target?

Below 3.5 at BU begins to limit program options. Strong extracurriculars, test scores, and compelling essays can compensate to a meaningful degree in BU's holistic process. Applying early decision provides a statistical advantage if BU is your first choice.

Improving your GPA once enrolled

BU's semester system and large course offerings mean students have significant flexibility in managing their academic load. Tracking grades carefully and identifying at-risk courses early is key to avoiding GPA problems that compound over multiple semesters.
