Admissions context for UNC Chapel Hill

UNC Chapel Hill is one of the most prestigious public universities in the country. The 19% overall acceptance rate puts it among the most selective public universities, though the rate varies dramatically between in-state North Carolina residents (who receive a significant preference) and out-of-state applicants. Out-of-state admission rates are typically well below 10%, making UNC one of the hardest public schools in the country to get into from out of state.

GPA by program

College of Arts and Sciences (in-state): Competitive but more accessible for NC residents. 3.8+ weighted with strong coursework typical.

Kenan-Flagler Business (direct admit): Highly competitive. Most business students apply internally after completing prerequisite courses. Direct freshman admission is very rare.

Gillings School of Global Public Health and Nursing: Competitive. 3.8-3.9+ unweighted typical for health-related programs.

Hussman School of Journalism: Competitive. Portfolio and writing samples matter alongside academic performance.

What if my GPA is below the target?

For in-state residents below 4.0 weighted, UNC becomes a reach but not impossible. Strong essays, extracurriculars, and test scores carry significant weight. For out-of-state applicants, below 4.3 weighted makes UNC a significant reach. Have strong alternative schools prepared.

Improving your GPA during college

UNC's academic reputation is earned through rigorous coursework. The pre-med population at UNC is large and competitive, which affects grading curves in biology, chemistry, and related courses. Structured preparation and grade tracking from day one are essential.
