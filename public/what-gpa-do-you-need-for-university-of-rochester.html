Admissions context for University of Rochester

University of Rochester is a research university with particular strengths in optics, brain and cognitive sciences, music, and engineering. The 33% acceptance rate is more accessible than many peer private universities, though the academic profile of admitted students is strong. Rochester's open curriculum — one of the most flexible undergraduate structures in the country — is a meaningful distinguishing feature that attracts a self-directed, intellectually curious applicant type.

GPA by program

College of Arts, Sciences and Engineering: Most programs in the main college are competitive with 3.7-3.9+ unweighted typical for admitted students.

Institute of Optics: World-renowned optics program. 3.8+ unweighted with strong physics and math performance through AP courses.

Hajim School of Engineering: Competitive. 3.7-3.9+ unweighted with strong STEM coursework.

Eastman School of Music: Separate audition-based admission. Academic requirements are competitive but the audition is the primary factor for music applicants.

What if my GPA is below the target?

Below 3.5 at Rochester is a notable challenge but more recoverable than at schools with 12-15% acceptance rates. Rochester's holistic process gives genuine weight to intellectual curiosity and fit with the open curriculum model. An applicant who demonstrates strong self-directed learning and genuine intellectual passion can be competitive below the GPA average.

Improving your GPA once enrolled

Rochester's open curriculum means students have significant flexibility in course selection but also more responsibility for their own academic planning.
