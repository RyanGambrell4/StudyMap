What It Does

Built for the kinesiology student's full workload

Six features that address the multi-subject demands of kinesiology programs.

Anatomy active recall

Muscle origins, insertions, actions, and innervations drilled with active recall, not passive table review. You produce the information from memory, which is what lab practical and written exams require.

Biomechanics problem sets

Lever systems, torque calculations, kinematic analysis, ground reaction forces. Timed problem practice in the format kinesiology biomechanics exams actually use.

Exercise physiology process maps

Cardiovascular response, metabolic pathways, training adaptations. Process map format so you can predict outcomes under changed conditions, the question type physiology exams use most.

Clinical application scenarios

Practice applying anatomy and physiology knowledge to clinical and sport scenarios. These appear on upper-division kinesiology exams and CSCS and ACSM certification content.

Certification exam prep

Integrate CSCS, ACSM, or NASM certification prep into your overall study schedule as a dedicated course. Practice questions aligned to each certification's content areas.

Multi-course load management

Kinesiology students often take anatomy, biomechanics, and exercise physiology simultaneously. The planner allocates hours by exam proximity across all courses so none falls behind.

Why kinesiology requires a different study system

Kinesiology does not fit neatly into one study approach. Anatomy needs memorization and retrieval practice. Biomechanics needs quantitative problem solving. Exercise physiology needs conceptual process understanding. Motor learning needs application to movement scenarios. A one-size-fits-all study app handles none of these well. StudyEdge AI adapts the session type to the course requirement.
