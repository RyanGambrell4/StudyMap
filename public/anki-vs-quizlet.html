The verdict: Anki vs Quizlet

Choose Anki if…

You're in medicine, law, or language learning, you need long-term retention for cumulative exams, you're comfortable with a technical setup process, and you're on Android or desktop where the free app is fully available. You need to create and share flashcard sets quickly with classmates, you study casually and don't need rigorous spaced repetition, and you're okay paying for features that used to be free. Quizlet is the easiest way to get a basic set of cards in front of you fast.

Frequently asked questions

Is Anki better than Quizlet for college students?

Anki is better for students who need long-term retention, especially in medicine, law, or language learning with cumulative exams. Its spaced repetition algorithm is more rigorous and it's free on desktop and Android. Quizlet is better for fast deck creation, easy sharing, and casual memorization. Neither builds a study schedule or structures your sessions — for that, you need StudyEdge AI.

Does Quizlet still have spaced repetition?

No. Quizlet discontinued its "Long Term Learning" spaced repetition feature in 2020. Its current "Learn" mode approximates spaced practice but does not use a true SRS algorithm. Anki still offers proper algorithmic spaced repetition for free on desktop and Android.

Is Anki free?

Anki is free on desktop (Windows, Mac, Linux) and Android. The iOS app (AnkiMobile) costs $24.99 as a one-time purchase. Anki has no subscription. Quizlet has a free plan with limited features and a Plus plan at $7.99–$35.99/month depending on billing period.

What are the downsides of Anki?

Anki's main downsides are its steep learning curve, outdated interface, and significant time investment to build well-formatted card decks. It has no AI features, no study schedule, no session structure beyond card review, and no grade tracking. It excels at spaced repetition but covers only one dimension of studying.
