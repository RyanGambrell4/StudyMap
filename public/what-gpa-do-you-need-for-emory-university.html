Admissions context for Emory University

Emory University is a highly selective private research university consistently ranked among the top 25 universities in the US. The 19% acceptance rate makes it competitive but not as extreme as schools like Vanderbilt or Georgetown. Emory is particularly strong in pre-medical preparation, business, and global health, which draws a highly qualified and motivated applicant pool. The average admitted student has a 3.8+ unweighted GPA with a rigorous course load.

GPA by program

Emory College of Arts and Sciences: Most accessible path. 3.7-3.9+ unweighted competitive with strong extracurriculars.

Goizueta Business School (BBA): Highly competitive. Direct freshman admission is limited and highly sought after. 3.9+ unweighted with quantitative strength and demonstrated leadership typical.

Oxford College of Emory University: A two-year liberal arts college that is an easier path to the full Emory degree. 3.6-3.8+ unweighted typical. Students transfer to the main campus after two years.

Pre-Medical Track: Very competitive. Pre-med students at Emory face rigorous science coursework and competitive grading.

What if my GPA is below the target?

Below 3.6 at Emory begins to limit options. Oxford College offers a lower-threshold entry point that provides a path to Emory credentials with a slightly less competitive admission process. For the main campus, below 3.6 requires strong compensating factors in other parts of the application.
