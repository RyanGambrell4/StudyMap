Michigan State University admits approximately 76% of applicants, making it one of the more accessible Big Ten schools. The average admitted student has an unweighted GPA around 3.5. Competitive programs like the Broad Business College and pre-med tracks are more selective.

The honest answer on GPA for Michigan State University

Michigan State's 76% acceptance rate makes it broadly accessible, but the quality of admitted students is still strong. The middle 50% of admitted students have unweighted GPAs between approximately 3.2 and 3.8. The average admitted student GPA is around 3.5 unweighted. Program-level selectivity varies: the Broad College of Business, College of Engineering, and College of Human Medicine (pre-med track) are all more selective than the general university admission rate suggests.

GPA by program

College of Arts and Letters / College of Social Science: Accessible. Competitive admits typically have 3.0-3.4+ unweighted.

Broad College of Business: More selective. 3.5+ unweighted typical for direct admission. Quantitative coursework strength is evaluated closely.

College of Engineering: Competitive. 3.6-3.8+ unweighted typical. Computer Science and Electrical Engineering attract stronger applicants.

Pre-Medical Tracks (Lyman Briggs College): Competitive. 3.7+ unweighted with strong AP science performance typical.

What if my GPA is below the target?

Below 3.0 at Michigan State limits options significantly. Between 3.0 and 3.3, general university admission is still possible but program selection is constrained. For competitive programs, below 3.4 requires stronger supplemental application components. MSU offers a pathway through community college transfer for students below the direct admission threshold.
