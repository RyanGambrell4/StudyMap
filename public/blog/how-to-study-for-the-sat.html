How to Study for SAT

The Digital SAT runs 2 hours 14 minutes, is adaptive at the section level, and covers Reading and Writing plus Math. Here is how to prep over 8 to 16 weeks depending on starting score and target.

Key Takeaways
  • SAT scores are highly coachable -- the exam tests a defined set of skills, not general intelligence, and those skills are trainable
  • Reading passages test your ability to find evidence for claims, not comprehend content -- approach each question as an evidence-location task
  • Grammar and writing questions follow a small set of testable rules (subject-verb agreement, parallelism, punctuation) -- learning these rules eliminates most wrong answers
  • Math without a calculator tests algebra and data interpretation, not advanced math -- speed and accuracy on basic algebraic manipulation is the bottleneck
  • Official College Board practice tests are the most accurate prep material; third-party tests vary significantly in difficulty calibration

What the Digital SAT actually tests

Reading and Writing is shorter passages with comprehension and grammar mixed together. Math is on-screen with a built-in Desmos calculator for every problem. The format rewards speed and pattern recognition over deep content; you have about 70 seconds per question.

Diagnose first

Take an official Bluebook practice test before any prep. The College Board's Bluebook app has 6 full-length digital SATs. Whichever score range you start in determines the realistic target and the prep length.

Reading and Writing strategy

Most questions are answerable from a short passage. Read the question first, then the passage with the question in mind. Vocab-in-context questions reward predicting the meaning before reading the choices.

Math: master the calculator

Desmos is built in. Learn to graph equations, find intersections, and use it for substitution checks. The students who never open Desmos leave easy points on the table.

Targeted drilling by subtopic

Pull 20 algebra problems, do them in a row. Then 20 geometry. Then 20 data analysis. Subtopic drilling builds the pattern recognition that mixed practice does not. A study schedule generator can map out your entire SAT prep timeline from today to test day automatically.

Vocabulary: smaller than you think

The Digital SAT cut explicit vocabulary questions. You still need a strong vocabulary for vocab-in-context items and for reading at speed. 200 to 400 high-frequency words is enough; do not over-invest.

Plan length by goal

Going from 1100 to 1300 in 8 weeks is realistic. 1100 to 1500 in 16 weeks is realistic with consistent work. 1100 to 1500 in 8 weeks is rare without intensive daily work.

Take full practice tests under realism

Once a week minimum in the last month. Full timing. Same time of day as your real test. Real morning, real food, real environment. The first time you simulate test conditions should not be test day.

Test day

Sleep 8 hours the night before, not just the week before. Walk in with the same routine you used in your best practice tests. Trust the prep; this is not the time to learn new techniques.
