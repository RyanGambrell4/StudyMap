Key Takeaways
  • Retrieval practice (self-testing) and spaced repetition are the only two study techniques with strong research consensus behind them
  • Highlighting and re-reading feel productive but produce no measurable improvement in exam performance (Dunlosky et al., 2013)
  • Interleaved practice (mixing topics within a session) produces better long-term retention than blocked practice, even though it feels harder
  • Elaborative interrogation (asking yourself why each fact is true) connects new knowledge to what you already know, deepening encoding
  • Concrete practice examples outperform abstract rule memorization for almost every subject -- work problems, do not just read solutions

Active Recall

Spaced Repetition

In-Class

Cornell Note-Taking

A structured note format that forces active review: cue column, notes column, and summary section, built for recall practice after class.

25-minute focused blocks with 5-minute breaks. Simple structure that beats marathon sessions for sustained concentration.
