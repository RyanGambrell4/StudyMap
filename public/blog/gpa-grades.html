Key Takeaways
  • Your GPA is a weighted average -- a bad grade in a 3-credit course hurts more than a bad grade in a 1-credit course
  • Grade replacement policies (where retaking a course replaces the original grade) vary significantly by school -- check yours
  • Going from a C to a B in most courses requires less work than going from a B to an A, so targeting C courses first is efficient
  • Office hours attendance correlates with grade improvement more reliably than hours spent studying alone
  • Many schools offer late withdrawals or incomplete grades for medical/emergency situations -- know the deadlines
