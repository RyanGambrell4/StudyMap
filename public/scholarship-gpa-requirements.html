How to protect your scholarship GPA

Most students lose scholarships not from one catastrophic semester but from a slow drift — taking on too many hard classes at once, underestimating workload, or not seeking help early enough.

Know your renewal terms before each semester

Read your award letter carefully. Most scholarships require both a minimum GPA and a minimum credit count per semester. Missing either can trigger a review or loss of aid — and many students only discover this after the damage is done.

A GPA hole is easier to avoid than to escape

Cumulative GPA math is unforgiving. A 2.7 semester when you need a 3.0 cumulative requires a 3.3 semester just to get back — assuming equal credit loads. One bad semester early in college can take years of strong performance to fully correct.
