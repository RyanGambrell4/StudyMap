The honest answer on GPA for Purdue University

Purdue's overall acceptance rate of approximately 67% masks significant variation by school and program. The overall university admits broadly, but applying to the College of Engineering, the Krannert School of Management, or the College of Science puts you in a much more competitive pool. Engineering in particular is where Purdue's reputation is strongest and where admission is hardest.

GPA by program

College of Liberal Arts: Accessible. 3.0-3.3+ unweighted competitive for most programs.

College of Engineering: Highly selective. Top 10 engineering program nationally. Admitted students average 3.7-3.9+ unweighted. Computer Science, Electrical Engineering, and Chemical Engineering are most competitive.

Krannert School of Management: Competitive. 3.5+ unweighted typical with strong math performance.

College of Science: Varies by major. Pre-pharmacy and pre-med tracks require 3.7+ unweighted with strong science courses.

What if my GPA is below the target?

For general Purdue admission, a GPA below 3.0 begins to limit options significantly. For engineering specifically, below 3.5 is a real obstacle even though the overall university is accessible. Students interested in engineering who do not make direct admission often enter through a different school and transfer internally after strong first-year performance.

Improving your GPA once admitted
