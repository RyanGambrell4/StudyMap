Built for Canadian Students

Canadian universities are competitive. Your GPA follows you everywhere after graduation.

Canadian students face unique pressures that most study apps were not designed around: bell curve grading at UofT, co-op GPA minimums at Waterloo, and a medical school pipeline that requires nearly a 4.0 to compete. StudyEdge AI understands what is actually at stake.

Med school GPA starts in first year

Canadian medical school requires a minimum cumulative GPA of 3.7 at most schools, and competitive applicants average 3.85 or above. That clock starts from your first semester. StudyEdge AI tracks your GPA in real time, shows your projected semester GPA, and flags which courses need more attention to protect your pre-med record.

Co-op placements depend on your GPA

At Waterloo and other co-op universities, your GPA determines which employers interview you and whether you qualify for competitive placements. Students in software engineering, mechatronics, or accounting co-op programmes cannot afford to let a term slip. Grade Hub tracks your GPA term by term so you see the trend before it becomes a problem.

Bell curve grading at research universities

UofT, Waterloo, and other research-intensive universities are known for aggressive bell curve grading in first and second year. A 75% in first-year physics at UofT might land at a B+ depending on how the class performed. StudyEdge AI helps you study for the actual exam standard, not just a generic percentage target.

Balancing jobs and part-time work

Many Canadian students work 10-20 hours a week to manage tuition and living costs, especially in Vancouver and Toronto where rental costs are high. StudyEdge AI builds your study plan around your actual hours, accounting for shifts and commute, so your academic performance does not have to suffer because your schedule is constrained.

Law, MBA, and professional school preparation

Canadian law schools start L1 GPA requirements at 3.5 and top programmes are significantly higher. MBA admissions at Ivey, Rotman, and Sauder are equally GPA-sensitive. StudyEdge AI helps you manage the undergraduate GPA that determines which programmes you can apply to after graduation.

Diverse student population, unified system

Canadian universities have large international student populations with diverse academic backgrounds. StudyEdge AI adapts to your courses, your schedule, and your study style. Whether you're a domestic student from Ontario or an international student from outside Canada, the system works the same way: your courses, your hours, your plan.

Frequently Asked

Questions from Canadian students

Is StudyEdge AI available for Canadian university students?

Yes. StudyEdge AI is a web-based app used by students at UofT, UBC, McGill, Western, Waterloo, Queen's, McMaster, U of A, and universities across Canada. The free plan covers one course; Pro is $2.99/week with a 3-day trial.

What GPA do Canadian medical schools require?

Most Canadian medical schools require a minimum GPA of 3.7 on a 4.0 scale, and competitive applicants typically have 3.85 or higher. Some schools use cumulative GPA, others weight the best two or three years. StudyEdge AI tracks your GPA on a 4.0 scale and shows your current standing, semester projection, and what grades you need to maintain your target.

Does it help with co-op GPA requirements?

Yes. Co-op students at Waterloo, UBC, Carleton, and other co-op universities must maintain a minimum GPA to keep their designation and access competitive placements. Grade Hub tracks your GPA term by term, flags when a course is at risk, and helps you allocate study time to protect your standing before the co-op office reviews your record.

Does StudyEdge AI work for the Canadian 4.0 GPA scale?

Yes. Most Canadian universities use letter grades (A+/A/A-/B+ etc.) mapped to a 4.0 or 4.3 GPA scale. Grade Hub tracks your GPA on the standard 4.0 scale, handles the percentage-to-letter-grade-to-GPA conversion used at most Canadian institutions, and shows your weighted average across all courses.

Does it help international students studying in Canada?

Yes. International students at Canadian universities often face additional pressure to maintain their GPA for study permit conditions or scholarship requirements. StudyEdge AI provides the same grade tracking and study planning for international students as domestic ones. The system adapts to your courses, not a nationality.

Does it help Canadian students applying to law or business school?

Yes. Canadian law school GPA requirements range from 3.5 to 3.9+ depending on school. Ivey, Rotman, and Sauder MBA admissions are equally competitive. StudyEdge AI tracks your GPA and helps you identify which semesters and courses carry the most weight in your admissions profile.
