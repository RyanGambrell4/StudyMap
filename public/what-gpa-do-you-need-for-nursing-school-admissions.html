BSN nursing programs typically require a minimum 3.0 GPA, but competitive programs at top nursing schools average admitted GPAs of 3.5 or higher. Your science GPA in biology, chemistry, and anatomy is evaluated separately from your overall GPA.

GPA requirements for BSN Nursing Programs

Nursing school admissions are more nuanced than a single GPA number suggests. Most BSN programs have a stated minimum GPA requirement, typically 3.0, but many competitive programs — especially those at universities with NCLEX pass rates above 95% — have average admitted GPAs in the 3.5-3.7 range. The most important GPA subcategory is science GPA, which covers your prerequisite courses: Anatomy and Physiology, Microbiology, Chemistry, and Biology. Programs evaluate science GPA separately because it predicts clinical performance more directly than overall GPA.

What competitive programs look for

Less competitive BSN programs (community colleges, regional universities): Minimum 2.5-3.0 GPA. Science GPA of 2.8+ competitive. Acceptance rates may be 50-70%.

Moderately competitive BSN programs (state universities): 3.0-3.4+ overall GPA. Science GPA 3.0-3.4+. TEAS or HESI entrance exam required. Acceptance rates typically 20-40%.

Highly competitive BSN programs (research universities, top nursing schools): 3.5+ overall GPA. 3.5+ science GPA. TEAS score 80+. Strong CNA or healthcare experience. Acceptance rates under 20%.

Accelerated BSN (ABSN) programs: Designed for non-nursing bachelor's degree holders. Typically require 3.0+ with completed science prerequisites. Very intensive programs with high attrition.

What if my GPA is below the average?

A GPA below the average for your target nursing program does not eliminate your candidacy, but you need to strengthen other components. A strong TEAS or HESI entrance exam score can compensate for a borderline GPA. Healthcare experience as a CNA, EMT, or medical scribe demonstrates clinical readiness. Repeated science courses with grades of A in the second attempt are evaluated — but one retake per course is usually the limit before it raises red flags.

Protecting your GPA throughout your pre-nursing track

The science prerequisites for nursing (Anatomy and Physiology, Microbiology, Chemistry) are memorization-intensive and require consistent review rather than cramming. Building spaced repetition flashcard systems for lab material and active recall practice for anatomy diagrams produces better grades than passive reading. Every tenth of a GPA point in science prerequisites matters for competitive nursing school admission.
