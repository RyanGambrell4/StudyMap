Admissions context for University of Virginia

University of Virginia consistently ranks as one of the top public universities in the country, and its admissions selectivity reflects that. The 24% overall acceptance rate puts UVA in a tier above most public universities and comparable to many private selective schools. The average admitted student has approximately 4.3 weighted GPA with an unweighted equivalent around 3.9. Course rigor is closely evaluated alongside the GPA number.

GPA by program

College of Arts and Sciences: The main path into UVA. Competitive admits typically have 3.8+ unweighted with the most rigorous course load available at their high school.

McIntire School of Commerce: Extremely competitive direct admission. Most students enter through another UVA school and apply to McIntire after two years, requiring a 3.4+ UVA GPA and a separate application.

School of Engineering and Applied Science: Competitive. 3.9+ unweighted typical with strong math and science preparation through AP courses.

Batten School of Leadership and Public Policy: Competitive. 3.8+ unweighted typical with demonstrated leadership and public affairs involvement.

What if my GPA is below the target?

Below 3.7 at UVA is a significant challenge given the 24% overall acceptance rate. A compelling personal narrative, exceptional extracurricular distinction, or legacy status can sometimes compensate. Out-of-state applicants face lower acceptance rates than in-state Virginia residents. UVA prioritizes Virginia residents significantly.
