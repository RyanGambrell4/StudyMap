The honest answer on GPA for Penn State University

Penn State has two tiers of admission: University Park (the main campus) and branch campuses. This page focuses on University Park, where the average admitted student has an unweighted GPA of approximately 3.6. The acceptance rate of around 56% is broader than Michigan or Florida, but program-level selectivity varies significantly. Schreyer Honors College requires much stronger credentials.

GPA by program

College of Liberal Arts: Generally accessible at University Park. 3.3-3.5+ unweighted typically competitive with reasonable test scores.

Smeal College of Business: More selective. Direct admission to Smeal requires 3.6+ unweighted with strong quantitative coursework.

College of Engineering: Competitive. 3.7+ unweighted typical. Computer Science and Chemical Engineering are among the most selective programs.

Schreyer Honors College: Highly selective within Penn State. 3.9+ unweighted with demonstrated academic distinction. Separate application required.

What if my GPA is below the target?

Below 3.4 at University Park, Penn State becomes a reach depending on the program. Branch campuses have significantly lower average admit GPAs (often 3.0-3.3 range) and are a viable path if University Park admission is uncertain. Many students start at branch campuses and transfer to University Park after strong first-year performance.

Improving your GPA once admitted

Penn State's branch campus to University Park pathway means that strong first-year college performance opens options that were not available from high school. A solid GPA in your first year of college at any Penn State campus can be a route to the University Park programs you want.
