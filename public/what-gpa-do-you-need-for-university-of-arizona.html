What GPA do you need for University of Arizona?

University of Arizona admits approximately 85% of applicants, making it one of the most accessible major public universities. The average admitted student has an unweighted GPA around 3.0-3.4. Honors programs and specific professional programs require stronger credentials.

Admissions context for University of Arizona

University of Arizona has a broadly accessible admissions process. The 85% acceptance rate means that most students with reasonable academic credentials are admitted to the general university. The practical question for most applicants is not whether they can get in, but which program they want to pursue and whether direct admission to that program requires additional qualifications. Honors programs and professional schools have higher thresholds.

GPA by program

College of Social and Behavioral Sciences / Humanities: Generally accessible. 2.5+ unweighted competitive.

Eller College of Management: More competitive than general U of A. 3.3+ unweighted typical for business programs. Direct freshman admission has additional requirements.

College of Engineering: 3.2-3.5+ unweighted typical with AP math and science coursework.

Honors College (University Honors): 3.5+ unweighted with ACT 25+ or SAT 1200+ typical. Significantly stronger academic profile required.

What if my GPA is below the target?

Below 2.5 unweighted, University of Arizona still has pathways through the Wildcat Gateway Program, which provides conditional admission with a roadmap to full enrollment after meeting academic milestones. Many students who would not qualify for direct admission use this pathway successfully.

Improving your GPA during college
