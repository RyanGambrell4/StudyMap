The Honest Breakdown

What each AI tool is actually good for in college

None of these tools are bad. They are just designed for different jobs. Using the wrong tool for the wrong task is why most students feel like AI is not helping them as much as it should.

ChatGPT

Best for: Explaining concepts in plain language, getting unstuck on a problem, brainstorming essay arguments, understanding a dense paragraph from your textbook. The raw capability for answering "what does this mean" or "why does this work" is unmatched.

Where it falls short: ChatGPT has no idea what courses you are taking, what is on your next exam, or when that exam is. It cannot build a study schedule, generate flashcards from your uploaded notes, track your grades, or tell you what you should be studying tonight. Every conversation starts from scratch with no memory of your semester. It is a general-purpose assistant, not a study system.

Perplexity

Best for: Research tasks that require cited sources. If you are writing a paper and need to gather information with references attached, Perplexity is excellent. It surfaces recent, sourced answers faster than a search engine.

Where it falls short: Perplexity is not built for studying. It has no flashcard generation, no study planner, no practice exam mode, and no grade tracking. It is a research tool, not an exam prep tool. Using it to study for an organic chemistry final is like using a library catalog to train for a race.

Notion AI

Best for: Organizing information you have already gathered. If you take notes in Notion, the AI can summarize them, extract key points, and help you format a study guide. For students who are already Notion-native, it is a useful layer on top.

Where it falls short: Notion AI does not build a study schedule, does not know your exam dates, does not generate practice questions calibrated to your course, and does not do active recall. It makes note organization easier. The actual studying is still entirely on you, and there is no structure to tell you what to study or when.

StudyEdge AI

Best for: College exam prep, end to end. You upload your syllabus, add your exam dates, and get a weekly study plan built around your actual schedule. AI generates flashcard decks from your notes. The practice exam feature generates questions calibrated to your course material. Grade Hub tracks every assignment and calculates what you need on the final to hit your target grade.

Why it is different: Every other tool on this list is general-purpose. StudyEdge AI was built for one thing: helping college students pass their exams. The tradeoff is that it is less capable at open-ended writing or research tasks than ChatGPT or Perplexity. But for the specific job of turning a syllabus into an A, nothing else does what it does.
