Why active recall actually works

The science behind it

The "Testing Effect" — documented by cognitive psychologists since the early 1900s — shows that retrieving information from memory is significantly more effective for long-term retention than re-reading or highlighting the same material. Every time you successfully recall something, the memory trace strengthens. Every time you fail to recall it, you identify a genuine gap to fix.

Students who use active recall retain material better, perform higher on exams, and finish with more time left over. StudyEdge AI builds it in automatically so you don't have to think about the method — just the material.
