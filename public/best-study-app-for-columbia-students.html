Why Columbia students need study management across disciplines

The Core Curriculum is one of the most distinctive undergraduate programs in the country, but it creates a study coordination problem: you're always working in multiple domains at once. A week might include a Literature Humanities discussion, a problem set for your STEM major, and a Contemporary Civilization paper. Each requires a different kind of preparation. Study tools built for a single discipline don't handle that well.

How Columbia students use StudyEdge AI each week

Add all your courses — Core and major — with their exam and paper due dates. The system builds a weekly plan that allocates session time by deadline proximity and subject type, rather than treating every course the same. You see clearly which course needs attention this week versus which can wait, and the plan adjusts as readings stack up or exams loom.
