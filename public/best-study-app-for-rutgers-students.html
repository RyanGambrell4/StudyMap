Why Rutgers students need to build their own study system

Rutgers' size and diversity are genuine strengths — it serves a huge range of New Jersey students from different backgrounds and with different goals. But with 70,000 students and a fragmented multi-campus layout, individual academic tracking is minimal. Large lectures are the default in introductory courses across virtually every program. Students who succeed at Rutgers — especially those aiming for competitive law schools, medical schools, or graduate programs — are the ones who take ownership of their academic progress from day one rather than hoping the university's systems catch problems early enough to matter.
