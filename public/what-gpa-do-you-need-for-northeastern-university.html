Admissions context for Northeastern University

Northeastern University has undergone one of the most dramatic selectivity transformations of any university in recent history. Two decades ago it had acceptance rates above 70%. Today it admits approximately 6.7% of applicants — placing it in the same difficulty tier as Georgetown, Vanderbilt, and Notre Dame. This transformation is driven by Northeastern's cooperative education (co-op) model and industry connections, which produce very strong employment outcomes for graduates. The average admitted student now has academic credentials that match any highly selective institution.

What if my GPA is below the target?

Below 3.9 at Northeastern is now a significant obstacle given the 6.7% acceptance rate. This represents a dramatic shift from even five years ago. Students who applied to Northeastern as a safety school should be aware that the current admit profile is very different from historical data.

Improving your GPA once enrolled
