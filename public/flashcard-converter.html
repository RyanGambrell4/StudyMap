How to make good flashcards

The tool handles the file format. The cards still have to be good. Three rules from the memory-science literature:

Keep the answer short

An answer that fits on one line is a card. An answer that fills a paragraph is a study guide. If you cannot say the answer in three seconds, split the card into two.

Ask real questions, not single words

A card with the front "mitochondria" is not testing you on anything. A card with the front "What organelle produces ATP through cellular respiration?" is. Complete questions activate retrieval; single words trigger recognition.

Combine flashcards with spaced review

Cards without a review schedule are just fancy notes. Import them into a system that shows them at spaced intervals (Anki, StudyEdge AI, Quizlet with the Learn mode). See spaced repetition vs cramming for the research on why.
