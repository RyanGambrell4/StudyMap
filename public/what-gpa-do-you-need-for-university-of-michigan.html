What GPA do you need for University of Michigan?

Average admitted GPA is approximately 3.9 unweighted (4.15 weighted). Michigan is one of the most selective public universities in the country, with an acceptance rate around 17%. The bar is different for engineering, business, and liberal arts.

The honest answer on GPA for Michigan

University of Michigan does not publish a minimum GPA. What it publishes is an acceptance rate that hovers around 17%, which is low for a public university. The middle 50 percent of admitted students have unweighted GPAs between approximately 3.8 and 4.0. Getting below 3.7 means the rest of your application needs to be exceptional to compensate.

GPA by program

LSA (College of Literature, Science, and the Arts): The most accessible path into Michigan. Admitted students average around 3.85-3.95 unweighted. Rigor of curriculum is closely evaluated alongside the GPA number.

College of Engineering: More selective. Most admitted students have 3.9+ unweighted with strong STEM coursework. Computer Science and Electrical Engineering are among the most competitive programs.

Ross School of Business (BBA): Extremely competitive. Freshman direct admission to Ross is one of the hardest admits in the country for a public university. Near-perfect GPAs are expected, and quantitative strength is heavily weighted.

School of Nursing: Competitive. 3.8+ unweighted is typical for admitted students. Science course performance is closely evaluated.

What if my GPA is below 3.9?

Michigan looks holistically. A GPA below the median does not automatically disqualify an application. Strong test scores (ACT 33+, SAT 1480+), compelling extracurricular achievement, and essays that demonstrate clear intellectual engagement can compensate to a meaningful degree. Apply Michigan as a reach if you are below 3.7 and a competitive reach at 3.7-3.85. Have strong backup schools prepared regardless.
